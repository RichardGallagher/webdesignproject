About Us

Ballyshannon rugby club is a small club based in South Donegal, we cater for all ages from mini to senior rugby, our home pitch is at the lakeside Ballyshannon. find out more information on our facebook PageMore information..

Head coach

Andy Doe

Andy joined the Ballyshannon coaching set up in 2016 as Assistant Coach. He was named as Head Coach to succeed Joe Doe after the 2019 Rugby World Cup.
Andy was assistant coach with blank RFCfrom 2011 to 2015 as well as defence coach for the 2013 Series-winning blank RFC tour to Australia.
Previous to joining the BlankRFC set-up, he coached Aviva Premiership side Saracens. A dual code player, he played rugby league for Wigan (370 caps), England (11 caps) and Great Britain (34 caps) before switching to union and joining Saracens.
Doe won eight caps for Irelnad, including three appearances at the 2007 Rugby World Cup.

Club president

John Doe

What an honour it is to be the President of Ballyshannon RFC for the 2019-2020 season and It is with great pride and delight that I take up this privileged post. Over the years we have had some remarkable people,
The Mini and Youth sections continue to flourish and as we all know, this is the life blood of our club. The under 14’s won their league and were unbeaten in 16 games. The under 16’s achieved the double, winning all 14 league games and claiming the Ulster Carpets Cup at the Kingspan Stadium. Our under 18’s finished runners up in the league and won the Nutty Krust tournament for the third year in a row.
I wish our coaches, team managers, physios, volunteers and supporters, along with all our players, who put their bodies on the line week
in and week out whilst wearing the RedBlue and Black, a memorable, enjoyable and successful season with City of Armagh RFC.

Yours in Rugby,

John Doe
